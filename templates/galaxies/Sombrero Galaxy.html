Sombrero

Looking like a broad-brimmed Mexican hat, the “Sombrero” Galaxy, M104, is a spiral galaxy seen nearly edge-on. The center of M104 is thought to be home to a massive black hole. This stunning Hubble image of M104, also known as the Sombrero Galaxy, is one of the largest mosaics ever assembled from Hubble observations.

Stars

    The Sombrero Galaxy (M104) doesn’t have specifically named stars due to its distance of about 31 million light-years. However, it contains an unusually large number of globular clusters—around 2,000—filled with older stars, making it a key focus for studying star clusters. The galaxy’s halo and bright central bulge host older stars, while younger, hot stars line the disk, creating its famous bright edge.
